“Marley was dead, to begin with.”

“马利已经死了——首先，这是确定无疑的。”

But on this Christmas Eve, it was not Marley’s Ghost that came knocking. At midnight, beneath the quiet winter sky, a small unsigned letter appeared at Anxin’s door. On its envelope were only these words:

但在这个平安夜，敲响安信房门的，并不是马利的幽灵。午夜时分，冬夜寂静。一封没有署名的小信，出现在安信的门前。信封上只有一句话：

“Come and listen to the nights your song has made warm.”

“来听一听，那些曾被你的歌声温柔陪伴的夜晚。”

That night, three Ghosts came to him in a dream.

那一夜，三位幽灵来到他的梦中。

The Ghost of Christmas Past

圣诞过去的幽灵

The first Ghost showed him Christmases Past. He saw a younger Anxin beneath the stage lights, singing his first songs. In the audience were family, teachers, and strangers hearing his voice for the first time. He did not yet know that his songs would one day accompany someone through a long winter—or accompany him through the more difficult years of his own childhood.

第一位幽灵带他看见了过去的圣诞。他看见年少的安信站在舞台灯光下，唱起最初的歌。台下有家人、老师，也有第一次听见他声音的陌生人。那时的他还不知道，自己的歌声有一天会陪伴某些人走过漫长的冬天，也会陪伴那个曾经有些辛苦地长大的自己，走过童年。

The Ghost of Christmas Present

圣诞现在的幽灵

The second Ghost showed him Christmas Present. Under the stage lights, Anxin gave his whole heart to every rehearsal, every performance, and every quiet moment shared with the people who supported him. Somewhere, someone was smiling at the same moment he smiled. Somewhere else, someone was listening to his voice, letting the night pass a little more gently. The joy he gave was never loud or fleeting. It moved quietly from one heart to another, becoming part of days he might never see.

第二位幽灵带他看见了现在的圣诞。聚光灯下，安信把全部的认真交给每一次练习、每一场演出，也交给那些与喜欢他的人共同度过的安静时刻。在某个地方，有人因为看见他的笑容而笑了起来；在另一个地方，也有人听着他的声音，让漫长的夜晚缓缓经过。他给予的快乐，从来不喧哗，也不短暂。它安静地从一颗心抵达另一颗心，成为许多他未曾亲眼见过的日子的一部分。

The Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come

尚未来临的圣诞幽灵

Then came the third Ghost, carrying no words, only a soft and distant light. It showed him Christmas Yet to Come. Beneath a great Christmas tree were gifts from every corner of the world: carefully written letters, familiar photographs, small handmade keepsakes, and wishes folded into envelopes. Anxin looked at the gifts, and at the letters resting among them. And he found that he had understood long ago. A gift is not made precious only by the moment it is opened. Its meaning grows as it is given, received, and gently passed from one person to another. A song can do the same. What begins with one voice may travel farther than the singer can imagine, carrying warmth, courage, and happiness to places he may never see. And perhaps that was the truest gift of all: to give happiness, and to discover that it had been quietly returning all along.

随后，第三位幽灵来到他的面前。它没有带来话语，只带来一束遥远而柔和的光。它带他看见了尚未来临的圣诞。一棵巨大的圣诞树下，放着来自世界各地的礼物：认真写下的信、熟悉的照片、亲手制作的小小纪念品，还有被折进信封里的祝福。安信望着那些礼物，也望着散落其间的一封封信。他发现，自己其实早已明白。礼物的珍贵，并不只在于被打开的那一刻。它的意义，会在赠予、接收与传递之间，慢慢生长。歌声也是如此。一首歌从一个人的声音开始，却可以抵达歌者无法想象的远方，将温柔、勇气与幸福送到那些他未曾抵达的地方。也许，这才是最珍贵的礼物：把幸福送给别人，也终于发现，那些幸福早已在漫长的岁月里，悄悄回到了自己身边。

And so, beneath the same sky, we gathered Anxin’s music, stages, memories, growth, and every heartfelt wish into one small gift.

于是，在同一片天空下，我们将安信的音乐、舞台、记忆、成长，以及每一份真心写下的祝福，收集成一件小小的礼物。

“God bless us, every one!”

“愿上帝赐福于我们每一个人！”
